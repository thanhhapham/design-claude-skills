# Carousell Brand Guidelines

*Source: Carousell Brand Guide*

---

## Brand Purpose

**"Share Possible"** — the brand idea behind everything Carousell makes. The platform enables people to share what they have, share possibilities with others, and make second-hand ownership accessible. Every design touchpoint should embody generosity, openness, and the idea that buying/selling secondhand is a positive, human act — not a compromise.

---

## Brand Personality

| Dimension | What it means in practice |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Inclusive** | Every user feels welcome — casual first-timer and power reseller alike. No elitism, no gatekeeping. |
| **Human-to-human** | We facilitate connections between people, not between users and a platform. The platform is a facilitator, not the star. |
| **Facilitator** | We enable the exchange, not control it. The tone is helpful and enabling, never authoritative. |
| **Win-win** | Both sides of the transaction benefit. Design and copy should not frame the buyer-seller relationship as adversarial. |

---

## Tone of Voice

| Quality | Sounds like | Never sounds like |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Warm and welcoming** | "Great news — your item sold!" | "Transaction confirmed." |
| **Direct and conversational** | "Add a photo so buyers can see your item." | "Please proceed to upload photographic evidence of item." |
| **Open and helpful** | "Here's how to price it right." | "You must follow these pricing guidelines." |
| **Hopeful and encouraging** | "Your listing is live — buyers are looking!" | "Awaiting buyer response." |

**Never:** preachy, corporate-speak, jargon, or condescending.

## Visual Identity

### Hero Colour: CarouRed
- Primary brand colour: `#E30613`
- Use for: primary CTAs, key brand moments, trust badges, important numeric highlights
- Never use CarouRed for error states (it will be confused with the brand colour)
- Use with restraint — it should read as a signal, not wallpaper

### Colour palette
- **CarouRed** `#E30613` — primary action, brand moments
- **Carousell Orange** — secondary brand accent (used in illustrations)
- **Carousell Yellow** — used in Explore and discovery surfaces
- **White/Light Grey** — primary backgrounds (clean, uncluttered)
- **Dark/Near-Black** — body text, primary information
- **Muted backgrounds** — used in cards and list items to reduce visual noise

### Typography: Fabriga
- **Fabriga** is Carousell's proprietary typeface. Used across all brand touchpoints.
- In-app, system fonts are acceptable for performance (San Francisco on iOS, Roboto on Android) — Fabriga is used in marketing and brand moments.
- Typography hierarchy: weight and size create hierarchy, not colour alone.
- Line height and letter spacing matter — dense text on mid-range Android screens reads poorly.

### Illustrations
- **Style**: Flat illustration with overlapping colours. Natural, approachable compositions — not sterile tech imagery.
- **Subject matter**: Real human situations — a person moving house, a family decluttering, two people completing a handover.
- **Colour use**: Overlapping shapes with semi-transparent fills create depth without 3D or shadows.
- **What to avoid**: Hyper-polished 3D renders, generic stock-style icons, overly abstract geometric shapes.
- Illustrations carry emotional warmth — use them in empty states, onboarding, and success moments.

### Photography
- Authentic, real, diverse. Not staged or stock.
- People-led: show real transactions, real moments.
- Product shots: clean backgrounds, natural lighting, multiple angles.

## Mr. and Miss Carousell

The brand mascots. Used selectively in playful, celebratory, and marketing contexts. Not used in core transactional flows. They represent the human, warm, approachable side of the brand.

---

## Brand Misuse to Flag in Reviews

- Using Title Case in interface copy (violates brand voice — too formal)
- Corporate or passive language ("Transaction has been initiated")
- Error states that feel punishing or accusatory rather than helpful
- Illustrations that feel generic, cold, or 3D/realistic (not on-brand)
- CarouRed used for error states (creates brand confusion)
- Tonal inconsistency between marketing comms and in-product copy
- Empty states with no illustration or warmth — missed brand moment

---

## AOP Alignment

Brand consistency is a trust lever. Across all 4 MWB pillars, users' willingness to transact depends partly on whether the platform feels trustworthy and human. Brand misalignment — clinical copy, cold empty states, inconsistent visual language — erodes that perception even when the core functionality is sound.
